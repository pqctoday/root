France's Role in Post-Quantum Cryptography

France, through its national cybersecurity agency, ANSSI (Agence nationale de la sécurité des systèmes d'information), is playing a leading role in promoting the transition to post-quantum cryptography (PQC). France is taking a proactive and multi-faceted approach to prepare for the impact of quantum computing on cryptographic systems.

Key Roles France is Playing

Supporting the Development and Adoption of PQC Standards

  • ANSSI actively participates in international PQC standardization processes, working closely with other national agencies like BSI (Germany) and NIST (USA).
  • They recognize the importance of adopting robust PQC algorithms that can withstand future quantum threats.

Promoting Hybrid Solutions

  • ANSSI advocates for the adoption of hybrid cryptographic solutions as a transitional approach.
  • Hybrid solutions combine existing (pre-quantum) cryptographic algorithms with post-quantum algorithms, providing an added layer of security during the migration phase.
  • ANSSI, like other agencies, acknowledges that standalone PQC solutions may be immature and recommends waiting for implementations based on final standards.

Providing Guidance and Recommendations

  • ANSSI provides clear guidance and recommendations to organizations within France for their transition to PQC.
  • This guidance outlines the steps involved in the migration process and assists organizations in making informed decisions about the selection and deployment of PQC solutions.

Establishing a Timeline for PQC Transition

  • Delivering the first French security visas for products implementing hybrid PQC (for both end products and intermediate products) by around 2024-2025.
  • Implementing hybrid cryptography by 2025-2030.
  • Moving to PQC-only solutions after 2030.

Promoting Research and Innovation

  • ANSSI encourages research and innovation in the field of PQC.
  • France invests in research efforts to advance the development of more robust and efficient PQC algorithms.

Collaborating with European Partners

  • France is actively involved in collaborative efforts within the European Union to promote a coordinated approach to PQC migration.
  • They are a co-chair (along with Germany and the Netherlands) of the NIS Cooperation Group's PQC workstream, which is dedicated to developing a roadmap for a harmonious transition towards PQC across the EU.

Focusing on PQC and Expressing Reservations about QKD

  • France primarily champions the adoption of PQC as the most promising solution to counter the quantum threat.
  • While they acknowledge the potential of Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) for specific niche applications, ANSSI considers QKD less mature than PQC.
  • France views QKD as a supplemental security measure, not a complete replacement for traditional cryptography.

France's active role in promoting the transition to post-quantum cryptography demonstrates its commitment to ensuring the long-term security of its digital infrastructure and economy. By actively participating in international standardization efforts, promoting research, and providing guidance to organizations, France is well-positioned to navigate the challenges of the quantum era.
