The United States' Role in Post-Quantum Cryptography

The United States has taken a leading role in promoting the transition to post-quantum cryptography (PQC) through various initiatives and actions aimed at mitigating the potential threat posed by quantum computers to current cryptographic systems. Here's a summary of the key roles the USA is playing:

Key Aspects of the USA's Role in PQC

Standardization Efforts

  • The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) has been at the forefront of PQC standardization.
  • NIST initiated a process in 2016 to solicit, evaluate, and standardize quantum-resistant algorithms.
  • This multi-year effort involved international collaboration and resulted in the selection of the first set of PQC algorithms in 2024.
  • The standardization process provides a framework for the development and adoption of PQC algorithms, ensuring interoperability and security.

Government Mandates and Policies

  • The U.S. government has issued several mandates and policies to accelerate the adoption of PQC across federal agencies.
  • National Security Memorandum 10 (NSM-10), issued in May 2022, set a goal of migrating federal systems to quantum-resistant cryptography by 2035.
  • The Quantum Computing Cybersecurity Preparedness Act further reinforced this directive, requiring federal agencies to inventory their cryptographic systems and develop migration plans.

Funding and Research

  • The U.S. government has been actively funding research and development in PQC.
  • The National Security Agency (NSA) has played a significant role in supporting PQC research and development, recognizing the potential threat to national security.
  • The U.S. Department of State has funded initiatives like the Post-Quantum Cryptography in the Indo-Pacific Program (PQCIP), which aims to build capacity and understanding of PQC in the Indo-Pacific region.

Collaboration with Industry and International Partners

  • The U.S. government is working closely with industry partners and international organizations to foster collaboration and knowledge sharing in the field of PQC.
  • This includes engaging with technology providers, standards organizations, and cybersecurity experts to ensure a smooth and coordinated transition to a post-quantum world.
  • The USA has also been working with its allies to develop international standards and guidelines for PQC, aiming to ensure interoperability and global cybersecurity.

Guidance and Resources

  • U.S. government agencies, such as CISA, NIST, and NSA, have been providing guidance and resources to assist organizations in their transition to PQC.
  • These resources include publications, fact sheets, and online tools to help organizations understand the quantum threat, assess their vulnerabilities, and develop migration strategies.
  • These efforts aim to raise awareness and provide practical guidance for implementing PQC across various sectors.

Through these initiatives, the U.S. is actively promoting the development, standardization, and adoption of post-quantum cryptography, aiming to secure the nation's critical infrastructure and sensitive data from the potential threat of quantum computers. The USA's leadership in this field is crucial in driving global efforts to prepare for a post-quantum future and ensuring the long-term security of digital communications and information systems worldwide.
