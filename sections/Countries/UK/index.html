The UK's Role in Post-Quantum Cryptography

Take the Quiz

The UK, primarily through its National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC), is actively promoting the transition to post-quantum cryptography (PQC). Here are some key aspects of its role:

Key Aspects of the UK's Role

Prioritizing a Timely Migration

  • The UK government recognizes the urgency of transitioning to quantum-safe systems.
  • It underscores the importance of beginning this transition as soon as possible to mitigate the threat of "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks.
  • UK-NCSC encourages a swift migration to quantum-safe algorithms but advises against using algorithms that have not yet been standardized.
  • It forecasts that some experts believe the quantum threat could emerge within the next decade.

Supporting the Development and Adoption of PQC Standards

  • The UK is actively participating in international PQC standardization processes, particularly the one led by the US National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).
  • UK researchers have played a significant role in NISTâ€™s process.
  • The UK generally recommends waiting for the finalization of PQC standards by NIST before deploying production systems.

Issuing Guidance and Recommendations

  • The NCSC provides guidance and recommendations to organizations to assist them in their PQC migration. This includes:
    • White papers and reports: Materials outlining the quantum threat, the principles of PQC, and practical steps for organizations to prepare for the transition.
    • Advice on Algorithm Selection: Recommending specific algorithms such as ML-KEM-768 and ML-DSA-65 for most use cases, emphasizing the need to wait for implementations based on final NIST standards.

Highlighting Migration Challenges

  • Identifying key challenges, particularly those related to legacy systems that are difficult to upgrade.
  • Challenges in sector-specific protocols and protocols operating on resource-constrained devices.

Advocating for Cryptographic Agility

  • UK-NCSC acknowledges the evolving nature of PQC and stresses the importance of cryptographic agility.
  • This involves designing systems that can be easily adapted to accommodate new cryptographic algorithms and standards as they become available.

Collaborating with International Partners

  • The UK actively collaborates with other countries and organizations to ensure a globally coordinated approach to PQC migration.
  • It collaborates with organizations such as NIST, BSI (Germany), CCCS (Canada), and NL NCSC (Netherlands) to exchange insights and best practices.
  • The UK is also a part of the DGX cybersecurity working group, which aims to facilitate the exchange of information and experiences regarding PQC transition.

Supporting PQC Research and Development

  • The UK continues to invest in PQC research and development.
  • This includes supporting academic research projects and encouraging industry innovation in the field of quantum-safe cryptography.

Overall, the UK is taking a proactive and collaborative approach to the transition to post-quantum cryptography. The government, through the NCSC, is actively engaging with national and international partners, promoting the adoption of standards, providing guidance, and supporting research efforts to ensure a smooth and secure migration to a quantum-resistant future.
