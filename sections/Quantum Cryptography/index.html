Quantum Random Number Generation

Quantum random number generation (QRNG) leverages the inherent unpredictability of quantum processes to produce truly random numbers. This is crucial for cryptographic applications where the quality of randomness directly impacts security.

Benefits

  • True randomness: Unlike classical methods, QRNG provides numbers that are fundamentally unpredictable.
  • Enhanced security: High-quality randomness improves the strength of cryptographic keys.

Limitations

  • Complexity: Implementing QRNG requires sophisticated quantum hardware.
  • Cost: The technology is currently expensive and not widely accessible.

Quantum Key Distribution

Quantum key distribution (QKD) uses quantum mechanics to securely exchange cryptographic keys between parties. It ensures that any eavesdropping attempts can be detected, providing a high level of security.

Benefits

  • Unconditional security: QKD is theoretically secure against any computational attack.
  • Detection of eavesdropping: Any interception attempt can be detected, ensuring the integrity of the key exchange.

Limitations

  • Distance limitations: The effectiveness of QKD decreases over long distances without repeaters.
  • Infrastructure requirements: QKD requires specialized equipment and infrastructure, which can be costly and complex to implement.
